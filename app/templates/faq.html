FAQ

How is this service supposed to be used?

In short:
  • Upload your GoldenCheetah metrics export file.
  • Start your Fitness Fatigue and your PerPot model fitting.
  • Generate your Fitness Fatigue and your PerPot plans.

What's the purpose of this project?

This project is part of a master thesis in computer science at the Bonn-Rhein-Sieg University of Applied Sciences. It's embedded in a larger research project called STELLA. The goal is to evaluate, if training models can be used with real world data not collected in a lab environment and if automatically generated plans based on these models can help cyclists to reach their goals.

What is a training model?

In our case a training model is a mathematical defined construct that tries to simulate how the human body responds to training loads. It takes some individual model parameters and a series of training loads as input and returns a series of performance numbers that should be close to the performance adaptation in reality.

What is a parameter fitting?

The process of parameter fitting tries to find the individual model parameters that let the model simulate reality as close as possible. The fitting can be done with a numerical technique like L-BFGS-B or other optimization techniques.

Why do you need my GoldenCheetah metrics export?

We use the performance and training load numbers for the individual parameter fitting.

How can I create a GoldenCheetah metrics export file?

To export your metrics from GoldenCheetah, go to the menu item "Export Metrics as CSV...". Depending on your version of GC, it may be located in the "Activity" or "Tools" menu and you may have to switch to the "Activities" view first.

How do you generate the training plans?

We use evolutionary techniques like CMA-ES or Differential Evolution. More information will be published in a paper in September as part of the INISTA conference proceedings.

Don't you think a training plan needs more than just a series of load numbers?

We are aware of the short comings of the models and our approach. But we still think it's worth to evaluate these models with real world data as a good model can be helpful in the design of a personal training plan.

The generated plan reaches my goal but the modeled performance changes during the plan are pretty unrealistic. What's wrong?

The models aren't perfect and are highly dependent on the individual parameters. So the model could be at fault or your parameters were fitted in a bad way. Like mentioned above, we are doing this to find such flaws.

Where can I get more information about the used models?

Read about Fitness Fatigue here. Read about PerPot here. Read a comparison of the two models here. More papers can be found with the usual search engines.

Why do my modeled start performances for a plan differ between the used models?

The two used models differ a lot in their definition and the quality of the result of the parameter fitting process may also differ from model to model. So differences in the modeled performance values are to be expected.
